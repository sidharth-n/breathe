Chandra Bhedana Meditation

What It Is and How It Works

Chandra Bhedana Pranayama (also called Moon-Piercing Breath) is a yogic breathing technique known for its cooling and calming effects. "Chandra" stands for the Moon and represents lunar, nurturing energy.

How It Works

The technique involves inhaling through the left nostril, as this helps to clear any blockages in the Ida Nadi, which begins in Muladhara Chakra and ends in the left nostril. Ida Nadi governs the parasympathetic nervous system, and is therefore related to calmness and relaxation.

The Basic Practice:

Sit in a relaxed posture, either on the floor with legs folded or on a chair with your feet touching the floor. Keep your spine erect and do not lean on the chair.

  • Close your right nostril with your right thumb (or use a nose plug if available). The right nostril remains closed throughout the entire practice.
  • Inhale: Slowly and deeply through your left nostril for 15 seconds.
  • Hold (Antar Kumbhaka): With the right nostril still closed, retain the breath (effectively closing the left nostril as well by holding) for 15 seconds.
  • Exhale: Slowly and completely through your left nostril for 15 seconds (right nostril remains closed).
  • Hold (Bahya Kumbhaka): With the right nostril still closed, remain without breath (after full exhalation through the left) for 15 seconds.
  • This completes one cycle. Repeat for the desired duration (e.g., 31 minutes as per the timer).

General Benefits for Everyone

Physical Benefits:

  • Enhances lung capacity: Slower, more controlled breathing of Chandra Bhedana naturally increases lung health over time.
  • Reduced heart rate and lower blood pressure.
  • Increases the coolness in the body because on inhalation, the air is filled with cooling energy.

Mental and Emotional Benefits:

  • Promotes relaxation, reduces stress and anxiety, and improves sleep quality.
  • Promotes relaxation, mental clarity, and emotional balance.
  • Activates the right hemisphere of the brain and helps tap into the parasympathetic system. It's suited for times of stress, distraction, agitation, and overwhelm.
  • Helps regulate the body's natural cycles, such as sleep and digestion.
